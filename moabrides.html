Klondike Area

KLONDIKE AREA - Still Investigating — This is a classic blue loop with red-level spice, offering slickrock, flow, technical climbs, and expansive views of Arches NP.

Basic Stats

  • Distance: ~12.5 miles loop
  • Climbing: ~1,200 ft
  • Tech level: Intermediate with advanced sections
  • Ride time: 2–3 hours (more with detours)
  • Best direction: Clockwise (Climb Mega Steps, descend Dino Flow / EKG variants)

🔄 Trail Weaving Options

🔺 1. Nome → Alaska → Klondike Bluff Loop

  • Entry: Start on Dino Flow south > Nome
  • Nome: Tight, technical, and climbing-focused; remote feel
  • Alaska: More flowy but still techy; connects beautifully into the climb toward Klondike Bluffs
  • Rejoin: Merge into Mega Steps or Dino Flow to head north and start the main loop
  • 🔥 Use case: Stronger riders wanting an engaging, remote-feeling lead-in with tech.

🔺 2. EKG South > EKG North as the Spine

  • EKG South: Chunky and exposed. Test piece.
  • EKG North: Still tough, but more compact and fast-moving
  • Option: Use EKG South from the bottom trailhead, merge into Alaska, climb into the bluff loop, then finish on EKG North back.
  • 🔥 Use case: Punishing loop for technical riders. High concentration of ledges, slickrock, and line choice.

🔺 3. Combine all for a Big Figure 8

  • Route: Park at South Klondike Trailhead
  • Up Dino Flow > Nome > Alaska
  • Into Mega Steps > Klondike Bluff Loop (main climb)
  • Drop down Baby Steps North > Baby Steps South
  • Connect to EKG North > EKG South
  • Finish on Dino Flow
  • Mileage: 22–24 miles
  • Climbing: 2,400–2,800 ft
  • 🔥 Use case: All-day ride. Max tech. Scenic. Bailouts possible via Baby Steps or Dino Flow.

🧠 Key Traits & Notes

  • Mega Steps: Best climb. Steps are challenging but doable. Beautiful transitions between slickrock and sand.
  • Klondike Bluff Overlook: Spur option—ride out to the overlook. Worth it for the Arches NP view.
  • Descent Options:
    • Baby Steps North to South: Fast and varied, mixes terrain types.
    • EKG: Harder, chunkier, more physical.
  • Nome & Alaska: Less traveled = more primitive; sharp, technical switchbacks; great connectors, but not beginner-friendly
  • EKG: If you want Moab ledgy gnar, EKG is the spine. Physical. Stay sharp—falls here have consequences. Best run north to south if only doing one half. But full out-and-back is epic.

🧩 Strategic Planning Tips

  • Water: No sources. Bring 2–3L per rider minimum.
  • Sun: Zero shade. Mid-day can be punishing.
  • Trailheads: South Klondike TH is best for big loops. Dino TH works if you’re staying south.
  • Bailouts: Dino Flow is your escape hatch at nearly any point. Keep it in mind.

🚴‍♂️ Example Itineraries

  • ⚡️Half Day “Core Tech”
    • Route: Up Mega Steps > Klondike Bluff Loop > down Baby Steps North > Dino Flow return

  • 🔥 Full-Day “Max Gnar”

    • Time: 4–6 hrs
    • Effort: High. Epic.
