Secret Stash Trails

Moab locals often discuss a handful of “under the radar” singletracks that offer a well-rounded ride (climbs, tech, and descents) away from the crowds and e-bike traffic. These trails are typically omitted from mainstream guidebooks and apps, and riders often keep them quiet. Below are several such trails, with details gathered from local forums and community posts:

Hidden Valley / Moab Rim Loop

  • Trailhead: Hidden Valley Trailhead (off Hwy-191 at Angel Rock Rd, south end of Moab)
  • Length: ~6.5–7 miles round-trip (loop or out-and-back)
  • Elevation: ~1,000 ft of climbing (mostly hike-a-bike), ~1,600+ ft descent
  • Terrain: Steep hike-a-bike (16% grade), secluded mesa singletrack, technical rim descent, 4x4 slickrock section, rock gardens, loose shale, exposure
  • Notes: Low traffic, ancient petroglyphs, no e-bikes (Wilderness Study Area), not listed on most apps. Recommended for experienced riders seeking solitude.
  • Sources: MTBProject.com, NSMB.com

Pipe Dream Trail

  • Trailhead: Southern end at Hidden Valley TH, northern end at Aspen Ave near Moab HS
  • Length: 4.8 mi one-way (9.6 mi out-and-back), ~3.2 mi of connectors
  • Elevation: ~1,650 ft gain (south-to-north)
  • Terrain: Constant climbs and descents (“saw blade” profile), tight bench-cut singletrack, shale, punchy climbs, narrow turns, variable surfaces
  • Notes: Local favorite, feels remote despite proximity to town, no official e-MTB access (BLM non-motorized), challenging and scenic
  • Sources: DiscoverMoab.com

Prospector Trail (Moab Rim Loop)

  • Trailhead: Behind Moab Rock Shop (600 N. Main), or by Ravens Rim Zip Line (north Moab)
  • Length: ~1 mi one-way; ~2 mi figure-8 loop possible
  • Terrain: Steep hillside trail, upper line is narrow/exposed, lower line is intermediate-friendly, packed dirt and slickrock
  • Notes: Built through private land partnership, not listed on major apps, local “secret stash,” no e-bikes, shared with hikers
  • Sources: MoabHappenings.com

Fisher Mesa Trail

  • Trailhead: Fisher Mesa overlook (via UT-128 > Onion Creek Rd, high-clearance vehicle recommended)
  • Length: ~8–10 mi one-way (16–20 mi round-trip), ~2,000+ ft descent/climb
  • Terrain: Remote mesa-edge singletrack, slickrock ledges, loose rock, fast hardpack, scenic but physically demanding
  • Notes: Secluded and beautiful, no maintenance, no e-bikes, excellent spring/fall ride
  • Sources: MountainBikeBill.com, Singletracks.com

“La Sal Backside” Singletrack (Dark Canyon to Beaver Mesa)

  • Trailhead: Dark Canyon Lake (La Sal Mountains), ends near Beaver Mesa/CO border
  • Length: ~14 mi one-way, ~3,500 ft descent
  • Terrain: Alpine start > meadows > slickrock > desert singletrack, continuous descent with ecosystem transitions
  • Notes: Shuttle ride, non-motorized, rarely ridden, needs navigation skills, pristine wilderness vibe
  • Sources: Singletracks.com

Each of these trails provides a balanced ride in its own way – whether it’s earning your turns with a tough climb (or hike) followed by a thrilling descent, or a rolling trail that mixes ups and downs. They’re cherished by Moab’s veterans and often intentionally kept off popular apps, so finding information requires digging through forum posts and local knowledge. The links and sources above point to the discussions and descriptions where Moab riders have shared these semi-secret spots. Enjoy the adventure, and as always, tread lightly to keep these special trails in good shape for those who follow!
